Generate a practical, action-oriented insight brief that can help content planning, creative direction, and topic selection.

- Do not claim real-world research unless actual source material is provided.
- Do not fabricate sources, statistics, competitor facts, market reports, or trending claims.
- If no real source data is provided, produce a strategic insight brief based on realistic market patterns, content behavior, and commercially useful assumptions.
- Keep the wording honest and non-fabricated.

Return STRICT JSON only.

PRIMARY GOAL:
Deliver a brief that helps the team decide:
- what kinds of content angles matter now
- what competitors or adjacent brands are doing conceptually
- what the tenant can adapt in a smarter way
- what concrete draft ideas should be created next

SUMMARY RULES:
- Must be practical
- Must be commercially relevant
- Must be concise but useful
- Should reflect tenant industry and audience
- Should not pretend to cite external research unless such inputs are actually provided

TRENDS RULES:
- Include trends that are realistic and strategically useful
- Prefer trends in:
  - content style
  - buyer attention
  - messaging angles
  - trust-building
  - educational content
  - short-form video behavior
  - proof / process / case-based storytelling
  - operational clarity
  - transformation stories
  - convenience / efficiency / automation / service quality themes when relevant
- Each trend must answer:
  - what it is
  - why it matters
  - how content can use it

COMPETITOR ANGLES RULES:
- Do not fabricate specific competitor names unless provided
- Treat competitorAngles as strategic angle analysis, not false factual reporting
- Focus on:
  - common angle competitors use
  - what is worth learning from it
  - what should be avoided
- Prefer insights such as:
  - overused promises
  - weak differentiation
  - better hooks
  - more useful education
  - stronger visual consistency
  - clearer proof structure
  - better CTA discipline

DRAFT IDEAS RULES:
- Must be actionable
- Must fit the tenant’s business
- Must fit likely audience interests
- Must be realistic to turn into actual drafts
- Mix formats where useful
- Prefer strong hook quality
- Prefer business-relevant and commercially useful themes
- Avoid generic filler ideas

QUALITY BAR:
- practical insights
- no fake sources
- no fabricated statistics
- no invented competitor claims
- actionable ideas
- tenant-relevant
- commercially usable
- suitable for content planning meetings or AI HQ review

STRICT OUTPUT RULES:
- Return STRICT JSON only
- No markdown
- No explanations
